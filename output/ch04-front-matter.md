# Chapter 4: Density Bonus & ADU Law
## Executive Summary, Key Takeaways, and Action Checklist

---

::: execsummary

California's Density Bonus Law and ADU legislation represent two of the highest-leverage tools for residential developers to maximize unit yield and financial returns on constrained urban sites. The Density Bonus Law mandates that cities provide increased density and concessions (parking reduction, height increase, setback relaxation) when projects include affordable units—with a 50% bonus at 15% very-low-income units, and an extraordinary 80% bonus for 100% affordable projects. Government Code §65915 makes concession denials nearly impossible: the city must prove a specific, unmitigable health/safety impact, a standard almost never met. ADU law has evolved from a 2017 opt-in to a 2025 by-right mandate—any residential property can now build detached, attached, junior, or conversion ADUs with 60-day approval and no CEQA. Pre-approved ADU plans (AB 1332, effective January 2025) collapse the design-and-permit cycle from 12+ months to 4-8 weeks, with soft costs dropping from $15K–$30K to $3.5K–$5K. HCD-certified prefab modular ADUs (AIAadu, Abodu, Villa Homes) compress total delivery timelines to 3-6 months versus 9-12+ months for stick-built, making them the only scalable model for portfolio ADU builders targeting 10+ units annually. The case studies (Palo Alto's 57-unit density bonus, Menlo Park's 108-unit 100% affordable project, San Jose's pre-approved + prefab ADU yielding $550K–$650K in property value added) demonstrate that density bonus and ADU economics often transform projects that fail the Napkin Test into viable ventures.

:::

---

::: keytakeaways

- **Density Bonus = Leverage for Breaking Height/FAR/Parking Caps**: A 15% very-low-income set-aside yields 50% density bonus plus concessions—cities cannot deny concessions without written findings of specific adverse health/safety impacts (Government Code §65915(e)). For 100% affordable projects, the 80% bonus combined with unlimited waivers essentially lets developers redesign the entire development envelope. The burden of proof is on the city—come prepared with evidence showing your concession creates no health/safety issues, and you'll break through local density caps that would otherwise constrain the project.

- **100% Affordable Projects Are Near-Impossible to Deny**: An 80% density bonus (60 base units → 108 units) plus unlimited waivers transforms projects that would otherwise be undeniable. Combined with LIHTC tax credits, low-income housing tax-exempt bonds, and local subsidies, 100% affordable development is now the regulatory pathway with the lowest discretionary risk in California—provided your pro forma works at 100% affordability and 12+ month holding periods for bond finance.

- **ADU by Right with 60-Day Approval Timelines**: State law now mandates that any residential or mixed-use lot can build detached (1,200 SF / 16 ft height), attached (50% of primary or 1,200 SF), JADU (500 SF within existing, owner-occupancy required), or conversion ADUs with no discretionary review and 60-day processing deadline. Cities that impose barriers face HCD enforcement action. Bay Area rental ADUs generate $3,000–$4,500/month—garage conversion ADUs in high-rent markets like Palo Alto achieve the highest ROI because they avoid foundation and utility connection costs.

- **Pre-Approved Plans (AB 1332) Cut Permit Time 50% and Soft Costs 75%**: Every California city must maintain a pre-approved ADU plan catalog as of January 1, 2025. If you select a city-approved plan, the jurisdiction must approve or deny within 30 days (vs. 60 days for custom plans). Pre-approved plans eliminate the $8K–$20K custom design cost and 4-8 week architectural cycle—dropping total pre-construction time from 3–6 months to 4-8 weeks. For portfolio builders targeting 10+ ADUs/year, pre-approved plans are the only rational approach. Always check the target city's catalog before commissioning custom designs.

- **HCD Certification (Real Property) vs. Non-Certified (Personal Property) Makes or Breaks ADU Investment Returns**: Factory-built prefab ADUs must be HCD-certified to be classified as real property and appreciate with land value. Non-HCD units are personal property (like mobile homes) and depreciate. A San Jose prefab project using HCD-certified unit (AIAadu $210K all-in, 4-month timeline) added $550K–$650K to property value and generated 18.3% gross yield ($3,200/month rent), whereas a non-certified unit would have added minimal value. Always verify HCD certification before engaging any prefab provider—the difference between appreciating real property and depreciating personal property is the difference between building wealth and buying a trailer.

- **Prefab/Modular ADUs Compress Timeline from 12+ Months to 3-6 Months and Save $140K+ on All-In Costs**: Traditional stick-built ADU: $350K+ cost, 12+ months timeline, custom design/permit delay. HCD-certified prefab (AIAadu 650 SF 1BR from $180K, Villa Homes $199K, Abodu $226K): $210K–$250K all-in with installation, 3-6 months total. For portfolio builders, the prefab model eliminates the biggest ADU bottlenecks. The 8-month earlier rent start ($3K+/month × 8 months = $24K+ additional income) and reduced carrying costs compound to improve IRR by 500–800 basis points when modeled in Appendix G's Level 2 full underwriting.

- **SB 9 + ADU Stacking Can Yield 8 Units from One Single-Family Lot**: A Sunnyvale case study used SB 9 lot split (1 → 2 lots) + duplex on each parcel (4 units) + ADU + JADU on each parcel = 8 units ministerial, no CEQA, ~90-day timeline. SB 9 parcels are capped at 2 units maximum regardless of unit type, limiting but not eliminating ADU additions. Model both the SB 9 path and the no-split ADU path for single-family lots—sometimes keeping the lot intact and maximizing ADUs under standard law (no 2-unit cap) produces more units than splitting.

- **AIAadu (Bay Area) and TJH (Silicon Valley Infill) Demonstrate Scalable Production Models for Developers**: AIAadu's vertically integrated prefab + pre-approved plans model delivers 350+ SF studios from $120K to 850 SF 2BR from $240K with 3-6 month timelines, HCD-certified, and pre-approval partnerships in San Mateo County and San Jose. Thomas James Homes (TJH) operates a production homebuilding model with ~50 adaptable floorplans, guaranteed pricing, 12-month delivery commitments, and $400M dedicated debt facility, making single-lot infill teardown-rebuild financially viable at scale. For portfolio builders, these models are the operational benchmarks—they demonstrate how to achieve production-builder efficiencies on land-constrained, lot-by-lot infill development.

:::

::: checklist

**Before Site Acquisition:**

- [ ] Calculate base density allowed by zoning and run Napkin Test (Appendix G) for base scenario
- [ ] Determine if 5–10% affordable set-aside qualifies for 20–50% density bonus and run pro forma with bonus units included
- [ ] Evaluate 100% affordable pathway if local LIHTC pipeline exists—80% bonus often unlocks viability that base density fails
- [ ] Check target city's pre-approved ADU plan catalog (every CA city required to maintain one as of Jan 1, 2025)
- [ ] Confirm property's zoning allows ADUs by-right (residential or mixed-use per §65852.2)

**During Entitlement / Density Bonus Strategy:**

- [ ] Identify concessions/incentives to request (parking reduction, height, setback, FAR increase)
- [ ] Prepare documentation showing each concession creates no health/safety impacts (burden is on city to deny)
- [ ] Model density bonus scenario in Appendix G Level 2 full underwriting and compare to base scenario
- [ ] For 100% affordable: verify LIHTC tax credit pipeline, HCD financing programs, and 12+ month holding requirements

**ADU Development:**

- [ ] Confirm lot is residential or mixed-use (ADU by-right per state law regardless of local ordinance)
- [ ] Determine ADU type: detached (1,200 SF, 16 ft), attached (50% of primary, 4 ft side/rear setbacks), JADU (500 SF, within existing, owner-occupancy required), or conversion (existing structure, no size limit)
- [ ] Check target city's pre-approved plan catalog before commissioning custom design—AB 1332 30-day permit timeline saves ~30 days vs. custom plans
- [ ] For prefab ADU: verify provider is HCD-certified and real property (not personal property/mobile home classification)
- [ ] Compare all-in cost: prefab turnkey ($210K–$250K) vs. traditional stick-built ($350K+) including carrying costs and timeline compression benefits

**Scaling / Portfolio ADU Strategy:**

- [ ] For 10+ ADUs/year: identify prefab provider (AIAadu, Abodu, Villa Homes) with pre-approvals in target jurisdictions to eliminate per-project design costs
- [ ] Verify site access for module delivery (crane reach, street width, overhead clearances, gate width for 8–12 ft wide prefab sections)
- [ ] Confirm foundation and utility connection scope in fixed-price prefab quote (often excluded—clarify before committing)
- [ ] For SB 9 lot split + ADU stacking: model both the split path and no-split path in pro forma to determine which yields more units and better returns

:::
