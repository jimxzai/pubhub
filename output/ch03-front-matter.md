# Chapter 3: Housing Legislation & Developer Strategy
## Executive Summary, Key Takeaways, and Action Checklist

---

::: execsummary

California's housing acceleration laws represent the most significant shift in land use authority in decades—transferring power from local governments to housing developers through ministerial pathways that bypass CEQA, public hearings, and discretionary denial. This chapter decodes the complete legislative arsenal: SB 35/423 for multifamily ministerial approval in RHNA-deficient cities (90–180 day timeline vs. 2–3 years), SB 9 for 4-unit by-right development on single-family lots, SB 330's preliminary application that locks fees before design, and the Housing Accountability Act's $10K/unit penalty for improper denials. You'll master the 2025 CEQA reforms (AB 130's broad infill exemption, SB 131's near-miss limited review) that eliminate EIR costs on 70–85% of urban housing projects, plus SB 79's transit-oriented density override (120 du/ac at 75 feet near rail). Through Bay Area case studies—Palo Alto's 1,580-unit El Camino pipeline, Cupertino's Vallco transformation, Mission Development's 40-project teardown-rebuild model—you'll see these laws in action. The chapter integrates pre-construction sales strategy (TJH's three-channel model, DRE public report pathways) and IRC §1031 exchange mechanics for tax-deferred equity compounding. **Core principle**: housing projects now operate under a fundamentally different regulatory framework than hotels or commercial development—know which pathway applies to your project before site acquisition.

:::

---

::: keytakeaways

- **SB 35/423 is ministerial approval with teeth**: Qualifying multifamily projects in RHNA-deficient jurisdictions (most Bay Area cities) receive by-right approval in 90–180 days with no CEQA, no hearings, no discretionary denial—cutting entitlement timelines from 2–3 years to 6 months. Check HCD's annual determination letter before site acquisition.

- **SB 9 unlocks 4 units on any single-family lot**: Duplex pathway + lot split = 4 units ministerial, no CEQA, 60–90 day approval. Combined with ADUs, achieves 6+ units on lots previously limited to 1. The 3-year owner-occupancy requirement for lot splits is the key constraint—plan your hold strategy accordingly.

- **SB 330 preliminary application locks fees and standards before design**: File immediately upon site control to freeze development fees, exactions, and building standards—preventing mid-entitlement increases of $5K–$10K/unit common in Bay Area jurisdictions. Requires only site address, unit count, and footprint sketch—no architectural drawings.

- **AB 130 CEQA exemption eliminates EIR for 70–85% of urban infill**: Projects ≤20 acres on urban infill sites with no environmental constraints now bypass CEQA entirely—saving $200K–$2M in consultant costs and 12–24 months. If one criterion is missed, SB 131's near-miss limited review targets the single issue, not full EIR. Run the AB 130 checklist before defaulting to traditional CEQA.

- **SB 79 transit-oriented density override reaches 120 du/ac at 75 feet**: Sites within rail/BRT distance thresholds qualify for state-mandated minimums that override local zoning—unlocking 3–5x density increases near Caltrain, BART, VTA. Tier 1 (major transit): 120 du/ac within 1,320 ft. Map your site's tier and distance before running pro formas.

- **Housing Accountability Act carries $10K/unit minimum penalty**: Cities cannot deny housing projects complying with objective standards without written findings of specific, unavoidable public health/safety impacts. Attorney's fees go to prevailing plaintiffs. HAA's burden-of-proof shift means conforming projects have near-zero denial risk—a fundamental change from discretionary hotel/commercial approvals.

- **Pre-construction sales de-risk absorption and accelerate capital recycling**: TJH's model (350+ homes/year) sells at permit stage under DRE exemptions, eliminating spec inventory risk and compressing capital cycles to 8–12 months. For subdivisions, Pink Report enables marketing, Yellow Report allows binding contracts. DRE path adds 2–9 months—structure projects ≤4 lots for exemption when possible.

- **IRC §1031 exchange defers 37% combined federal + California capital gains**: Developers who hold properties as investment (12+ months minimum) can exchange into replacement property, deferring 23.8% federal + 13.3% CA tax—leaving ~37% more equity available for the next project. Separate build-to-sell entities from hold-for-investment entities to avoid dealer exclusion trap. Engage Qualified Intermediary before listing.

:::

::: checklist

**Before Site Acquisition:**

- [ ] Check HCD's SB 35/423 annual determination letter for target jurisdiction—confirms ministerial eligibility and affordability threshold (10% or 50% BMR)
- [ ] Map site's distance to Tier 1/2 transit stations (Caltrain, BART, VTA)—verify SB 79 density/height override applicability
- [ ] Run AB 130 infill CEQA exemption checklist—confirm urban infill status, site size ≤20 acres, no environmental constraints (§3.6.1)
- [ ] Pull General Plan land use designation and zoning—determine if ministerial pathway exists or if GPA/rezoning required (cross-ref Ch 1–2)
- [ ] For single-family lots: evaluate SB 9 (duplex + lot split = 4 units) vs. ADU pathway (AB 976 + AB 1033)—choose faster route

**At Site Control (Day 1):**

- [ ] File SB 330 preliminary application immediately—lock fees, standards, and exactions before they increase mid-entitlement (§3.3)
- [ ] For commercial-to-residential conversions: confirm AB 2011 eligibility (commercial corridor width 70–150 ft, prevailing wage)
- [ ] For small projects (≤10 units): check SB 684/1123 eligibility for 60-day ministerial approval on infill sites <5 acres

**During Entitlement:**

- [ ] If AB 130 exemption denied: evaluate SB 131 near-miss limited review before defaulting to full EIR (§3.6.2)
- [ ] Track Permit Streamlining Act statutory clocks (30-day completeness, 60/120/180-day approval deadlines)—send formal notice if city misses deadlines (§3.5.4)
- [ ] For jurisdictions with posted timelines (AB 2234): document delays beyond posted timelines for escalation leverage
- [ ] Confirm blanket encumbrance release or bond if accepting buyer deposits before certificate of occupancy (DRE compliance, §3.7)

**Pre-Construction / Tax Planning:**

- [ ] For projects intended for 1031 exchange: hold in separate entity from build-to-sell operations, plan 12–24 month rental hold period (§3.8)
- [ ] Engage Qualified Intermediary before listing any property intended for exchange—funds cannot touch developer's accounts
- [ ] If exchanging California property for out-of-state replacement: set up annual FTB Form 3840 filing protocol (California clawback tracking)
- [ ] For pre-construction sales: determine DRE public report requirement (≤4 lots or build-before-sale = exempt; else file Pink → Yellow → White)

**Title 24 2025 Compliance (Permits Filed 2026+):**

- [ ] Design for all-electric: heat pump HVAC, heat pump water heater, EV-ready garage, battery storage (BESS)—gas pool heaters no longer code-compliant (§3.6.6)
- [ ] For sites ≤500 ft from freeway: budget for MERV 16 HVAC filtration, no freeway-facing balconies (AB 205 air quality)

:::
