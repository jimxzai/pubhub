# Chapter 9: Franchise & Brand Standards
## Executive Summary, Key Takeaways, and Action Checklist

---

::: execsummary

Hotel franchise agreements are **fixed-cost structures that lock you into specific room dimensions, amenity standards, operating economics, and fee obligations** for 15–25 years. This chapter decodes the franchising architecture: initial fees ($50K–$100K), royalties (4–6% gross room revenue), marketing fund (2–4%), key money (forgivable loan of $5K–$20K per key in supply-constrained markets), and mandatory Property Improvement Plans every 6–7 years ($10K–$30K per key). You'll master brand selection strategy across five tiers—economy (Fairfield, Hampton: 300–350 SF, highly standardized, modular-ideal); select-service (Courtyard, AC, Moxy: 250–325 SF, pre-approved modular prototypes); upper-upscale (Marriott, Hilton: 375–450 SF, limited modular fit); luxury (Ritz-Carlton, St. Regis: 500–700+ SF, fully custom, no modular); lifestyle (citizenM, AC, Moxy: 150–250 SF, compact, modular-optimal). Three case studies validate the critical franchise-entitlement coordination: citizenM Menlo Park (240 keys, lifestyle brand's lean operating model at 0.3 FTE/key vs. 0.5–0.8 for traditional brands); Peninsula LOI expiration risk (24-month LOI expired during 30-month entitlement, forcing redesign, adding 8 months); Marriott Peninsula opportunity (no modular hotel built on Peninsula to date; first-mover advantage for franchise-friendly jurisdiction). **Core principle**: brand selection is your first major decision—it locks room size, construction method, target market, and fee structure. Choose select-service (Fairfield, Courtyard, Moxy) if modular is your strategy; lifestyle (citizenM) if you have operator expertise; luxury if capital is unlimited and entitlements are easy.

:::

---

::: keytakeaways

- **Initial franchise fee ($50K–$100K) plus royalties (4–6%) and marketing (2–4%) are mandatory operating drains**: These are not negotiable. On a 120-key hotel at $150 ADR, 65% occupancy, that's $3.29M gross room revenue annually. Royalty (5%) = $165K/year; marketing (3%) = $99K/year. Combined $264K/year in perpetual fees is 8% of total operating expenses. Model this in pro forma as permanent cost reduction from revenue.

- **Key money ($5K–$20K per key) is negotiable in supply-constrained markets and reduces equity required**: On a 120-key project, key money of $10K/key = $1.2M forgivable loan. This appears as a credit in the Sources section of the pro forma (Appendix G), reducing equity requirement. Negotiability is highest in markets where the brand is competing for deals (Silicon Valley for select-service, urban cores for lifestyle).

- **Property Improvement Plans (PIPs) every 6–7 years require $10K–$30K per key capital—budget in long-term reserve**: On a 120-key hotel, a $20K/key PIP = $2.4M renovation. This appears in the stress test (Appendix G §3) as a 6–7 year recurring capital reserve, not a one-time cost. Failure to pass PIP inspections can result in franchise termination.

- **Brand tier determines room size, modular suitability, and target market positioning**: Economy/Select-Service (300–350 SF, Fairfield/Courtyard) ideal for modular; Lifestyle (150–250 SF, citizenM/Moxy) optimal for modular due to compact repetition; Luxury (500–700+ SF, Ritz/St. Regis) impossible for modular. Choose brand before finalizing structural system; changing brand mid-design costs $500K+ in redesign.

- **Franchise LOI expiration risk (24–36 month term) is a kill-fast scenario if entitlement timeline exceeds LOI term**: Peninsula case study (Case 2) is textbook: 24-month LOI expired at month 24 of 30-month entitlement, brand declined renewal, developer forced to redesign for new brand. Entitlement slipped to 38 months total. Negotiate 36-month LOI with 12-month extension option; validate LOI expiration is 6+ months *after* projected entitlement completion.

- **Franchise approval contingent on site, market, and developer approval—engage brand at pre-application phase**: Brand development team must review site, market study, competitive set, and developer financials before issuing LOI. Franchising is typically month 3–6 of project timeline. Delays in franchise approval cascade into entitlement delays if not managed.

- **Select-Service brands (Courtyard, AC, Moxy) have pre-approved modular prototypes and Marriott key money incentives**: Marriott offers forgivable loan incentives for modular projects in target markets. Courtyard, AC, Moxy all have pre-approved modular prototypes. If modular is your strategy, these brands automatically reduce risk vs. independent brand selection.

- **Lifestyle brands (citizenM) operate with 0.3 FTE per key vs. 0.5–0.8 for traditional brands—dramatically different operating economics**: citizenM Menlo Park case study shows compact rooms (150–160 SF), no front desk (app-based check-in), no room service, self-service model. This 40–60% labor reduction creates superior RevPAR-to-cost ratios despite lower rack rates. Model operating expenses differently by brand tier; don't assume all select-service brands have identical labor costs.

:::

::: checklist

**Before Franchise Selection (Month 1–3):**

- [ ] Complete market study and competitive set analysis within 3-mile radius—identify white space by brand tier (is Courtyard already oversupplied?) and price point ($80–$120 ADR vs. $150–$200)
- [ ] Evaluate brand portfolio within franchise family—Marriott's Courtyard, AC, Moxy serve different segments of same demand base; key money availability varies significantly by brand and location
- [ ] Assess site market position: is this high-barrier-to-entry market where brands compete aggressively (Silicon Valley) or commoditized market where all brands available? Negotiating position differs 10–20x based on market dynamics

**Franchise Application Phase (Month 3–6):**

- [ ] Schedule brand development team meeting with site plan, market study, financial pro forma, and developer resume—brand reviews all before LOI issuance
- [ ] Secure Letter of Intent with **minimum 36-month term** and include 12-month extension option in LOI language—validate expiration date is 90+ days *after* projected entitlement completion
- [ ] For supply-constrained markets (Silicon Valley, urban cores), request key money negotiation at LOI stage—typical range $5K–$20K per key, structured as forgivable loan
- [ ] Confirm brand's modular prototype availability and pre-approval status—Marriott select-service brands all pre-approved; confirm dimensional compatibility with your structural plan

**Franchise Agreement & Fee Structure (Month 6–9):**

- [ ] Model franchise fees in pro forma (Appendix G §2C, Revenue section): royalty (4–6% gross room revenue), marketing fund (2–4%), initial fee ($50K–$100K), key money credit if negotiated
- [ ] Review transfer provisions and exit strategy implications—15–25 year term; resale typically requires brand approval; transfer fee typically 50% of current initial fee
- [ ] Confirm Property Improvement Plan (PIP) cycle—typically every 6–7 years, budget $10K–$30K per key; plan recurring capital reserve in stress test (Appendix G §3)
- [ ] Obtain copy of FDD (Franchise Disclosure Document) from brand legal team; review for any non-standard terms or performance covenants

**Construction Document Approval Phase (Month 12–18):**

- [ ] Coordinate brand technical services review of construction documents *before* building permit submission—brand must approve room layouts, MEP rough-in, FF&E specifications
- [ ] Confirm FF&E (furniture, fixtures, equipment) specification compliance—brand specifies mattresses, linens, art, signage, elevator finishes, lobby design; non-compliance delays opening
- [ ] Plan brand technical services inspections at key construction milestones (slab pour, rough-in completion, rough-in inspection, final inspection)—these run parallel to local building inspections
- [ ] Establish pre-opening protocol with brand: operating systems installation (PMS, POS, loyalty), staff training, mock opening, brand sign-off before revenue generation

**Operating Systems & Opening (Month 24–30):**

- [ ] Implement Property Management System (PMS) approved by brand (Marriott Bonvoy integration required for all Marriott franchises)—training for staff must be completed before opening
- [ ] Coordinate brand loyalty program enrollment (Marriott Bonvoy, independent brand program)—this must be operational before opening to capture day-one reservations
- [ ] Budget for brand training: staff orientation, front desk procedures, housekeeping standards, revenue management—typically 4–8 weeks pre-opening
- [ ] Schedule brand opening inspection and technical services final approval before certificate of occupancy—opening cannot occur without brand sign-off

**Risk Management & Long-Term Planning:**

- [ ] Create franchise expiration buffer calendar: LOI expiration date minus 90 days = entitlement completion deadline—if entitlement slips beyond this date, escalate to brand development team immediately
- [ ] Model PIP cycle in stress test (Appendix G §3): first PIP typically years 6–7, then every 6–7 years thereafter; budget capital reserve of $10K–$30K per key per cycle
- [ ] Review brand's market position annually: if brand is losing market share or eliminating category (Marriott discontinued Ritz-Carlton franchising), explore transfer options or franchise buyout scenarios
- [ ] Evaluate franchising vs. independent operation: Compare 5-year pro forma under franchise vs. non-franchise operation (citizenM model). If NOI difference exceeds $500K cumulative, franchising ROI is negative.

:::
